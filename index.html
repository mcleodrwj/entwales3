ENT Emergency Skills Course

Course Overview 


- Single day course for £30 including lunch.
- Learn how to manage common ENT emergencies
- Learn to recognise the situations when more senior help is required.
- Highly practical and interactive.
- Experienced and friendly faculty
- Based in Llandough Hospital Postgraduate department
- Only Royal College of Surgeons Edinburgh Accredited course for ENT
- Running for over 9 years
- Aimed at FP1-CT2 doctors who will be rotating through or crossovering ENT or Hospital at Night.
- Also designed for ENT and A&E Nurses. 
- Day includes five stations each with a practical element including: Ear, Nose, Throat, Airway and SimMan emergency simulation session.

Airway

• History and examination of paediatric and adult patients with stridor
• Acute management of stridor
• Principles of and indications for tracheostomy and laryngectomy • Practical scenarios in the management of patients with airway obstruction after tracheostomy and laryngectomy

Throat

• History and examination of throat and neck in adult and paediatric patients
• Management of tonsillitis, quinsy, epiglottis and neck swellings
• How to manage post tonsillectomy bleed
• Investigate and manage foreign bodies- the questions to ask

Nose

• History and examination of the nose using pen torch, otoscope and headlight/nasal speculum
• Assessment and management of a patient with epistaxis- perform nasal packing
• Examination and management of nasal trauma and foreign bodies
• Describe and accurately assess the potential complications of acute sinusitis

Ear

• History and examination of the ear in adult and paediatric patients
• Management of otological infections, trauma and perform techniques for removal of foreign bodies
• Managing a lower motor neurone facial palsy, acute sensorineural hearing loss and differentiate central from peripheral aetiologies of acute vertigo-‘the big 4’

SimMan

• Manage ENT emergencies in a simulated environment
• Put your new clinical skills into practice
• A time to consolidate learning
